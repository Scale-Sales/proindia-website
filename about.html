The Journey

From Chief Sustainability Officer to circular-economy founder

Amit spent nearly two decades at Hindustan Coca-Cola, rising to Chief Sustainability Officer for India & South West Asia. He built the company's sustainability, EHS, renewable energy and water-stewardship ecosystem across 45+ factories — and delivered over ₹1,200 crore in cumulative business impact through operational excellence, LEAN and Six Sigma.

In 2018, after a second MBA at ISB and executive programs at Wharton and Kellogg, he left to build Pro India — a social-but-for-profit enterprise taking circular-economy solutions to plastics, electronics, batteries and vehicles.

Today Pro India helps companies go beyond compliance — charting ESG and climate journeys with positive ROI, and building a unique learn-implement-repeat pedagogy for executives transitioning into sustainability roles.

Our Work

A body of work — proof, not claims

Industry forums, knowledge sessions, school awareness and grassroots CSR — the ground Pro India has actually covered.

Awards & Industry Forums

Convened the SANRAKSHAN Awards 2025 for sustainability leaders, featured at the FICCI 3rd Sustainable Industrial Practice Awards, and represented industry in CII, FICCI, ASSOCHAM & AMCHAM forums.

🎥
Knowledge Webinars

Live sessions hosted by Amit Saha — Evolving ESG & BRSR Reporting, E-Waste Rules 2023, and Battery Waste Rules 2023 — decoding new regulations for producers and brand owners.

🏫
School Awareness Program

Plastic-waste awareness and behaviour-change sessions across 16+ schools — DPS Gurugram, Shiv Nadar, JBCN International, Lotus Valley, Mount Carmel and more — engaging students aged 8–14.

🤝
Wastepicker Upliftment & CSR

A dignity-and-livelihood program for waste pickers — identity, capacity, enterprise and awareness — touching 14 of 17 UN SDGs, run through Pro India's Social Impact & CSR arm.

🌱
Net Zero Plastics & Plastic for a Cause

A plastic-footprint calculator that lets citizens and brands measure, neutralise (from ₹500/yr) and act — with collection routed through fairly-paid waste pickers.
